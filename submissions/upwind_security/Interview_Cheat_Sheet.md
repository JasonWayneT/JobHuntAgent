I want to bring that disciplined prioritization to a company that is actually defining the next era of cloud security.

## 4. Handling Ambiguity and Stability

In a hypergrowth environment like Upwind, the temptation is to say yes to every enterprise request. My approach to prioritization is built on "constraint discipline." 

At Cision, I operated with a shrinking headcount and high leadership turnover. I managed this by implementing a structured capacity model. We used T-shirt sizing with uncertainty bands and allocated capacity across three specific streams: security/stability, migration, and planned initiatives. If a request did not fit the stream, it was a "no." 

I do not wait for perfect data to make decisions. If the metrics are fragmented, I build the systems to track them. I have survived four rounds of layoffs and three CEO changes by staying focused on the business outcomes: protecting revenue and reducing churn drivers. I bring that same "calm under fire" mentality to the chaos of a scaling startup.
