# Interview Run of Show: Jason Taylor x Yeet

## 1. "Tell me about yourself" (60-90 Seconds)

**The Pitch:**
I am a Platform Product Manager who specializes in technically complex, revenue-bearing B2B SaaS environments. My career has been defined by sitting close to engineering and systems to drive business outcomes. Most recently at Cision, I owned the legacy C3 platform and the underlying CPRE infrastructure. I was responsible for stabilizing a platform tied to 40 million dollars in ARR and 3,500 accounts during a period of significant organizational churn and shrinking resources. 

I am not a 0-to-1 growth hacker. I am a pragmatic PM who excels at fixing the plumbing that keeps a business running. I have a track record of reducing churn by solving deep-seated data integrity issues and building migration tooling that protects revenue during platform transitions. I thrive in engineering-heavy cultures where the product's value is tied to its reliability and performance. I am looking to bring that discipline to Yeet to help scale your core systems and ensure your GTM efforts are backed by a stable, high-performance platform.

## 2. Why Yeet?

**The Connection:**
Yeet is clearly prioritizing high-performance engineering, evidenced by your focus on Rust and on-site collaboration in Chicago. My experience at Cision taught me that the most successful products are built when PMs and Engineers are tightly aligned on system architecture and data flow. 

I am drawn to Yeet because you are in a high-growth phase where technical debt and scaling bottlenecks can quickly derail GTM momentum. I have spent the last four years managing those exact trade-offs. While many PMs focus only on the UI, I focus on the ingestion pipelines and database flows that actually deliver the value. Your on-site culture matches my preferred operating style: solving hard problems in real-time with the people building the code.

## 3. Key Experience Bridge

**Data Integrity & Performance (The Bellwether Story):**
Yeet likely faces challenges with data freshness or system latency as you scale. At Cision, our biggest churn driver was stale contact data. I led Project Bellwether, which bypassed a failing legacy ETL chain to integrate directly with our upstream source of truth. This eliminated a 30 to 40 percent data drop-off and restored customer trust. I can apply this same architectural thinking to Yeet’s data pipelines to ensure your users are always working with the most accurate information.

**Infrastructure Modernization (CPRE & Kafka):**
Yeet is building for performance. I managed the CPRE infrastructure layer and scoped the transition from RabbitMQ to Kafka to improve scalability and throughput. I understand how to roadmap these technical migrations without breaking the existing revenue-generating features. I can help Yeet balance the need for cutting-edge performance (like your Rust initiatives) with the requirement for platform stability.

**Disciplined Prioritization:**
In a startup, everything feels like a priority. I developed a capacity modeling system at Cision that factored in PTO, uncertainty, and T-shirt sizing to prevent team burnout. I can bring this level of rigor to Yeet to ensure we are shipping the highest-impact work rather than just reacting to the loudest stakeholder.

## 4. Handling Ambiguity & Stability

**The Narrative:**
I have operated through four rounds of layoffs and three executive transitions while maintaining a consistent roadmap. When the support organization was disbanded, I built a structured ticket prioritization system in Jira to ensure my team was only touching the most critical, revenue-impacting bugs. 

**The Strategy:**
If Yeet is experiencing rapid shifts in direction, I provide stability by creating "initiative streams." I believe in saying no to distractions so the engineering team can focus on core platform health. I do not need perfect data to make decisions; I build the systems that make evidence-based product management possible. I am comfortable being the central subject matter expert who bridges the gap between a complex backend and a fast-moving GTM team.

## 5. Custom Reverse-Interview Questions

**To the CTO / Engineering Lead:**
*   You are clearly investing in high-performance tools like Rust. How do you currently balance the trade-off between shipping new GTM features and the necessary refactoring required to maintain that performance edge?
*   Regarding your data ingestion and processing: where do you see the most significant bottlenecks today, and how much of the PM's role here is expected to be architectural versus functional?

**To the CEO / Product Lead:**
*   Yeet has a strong on-site culture in Chicago. How has this proximity specifically accelerated your product-market fit or your ability to pivot compared to remote competitors?
*   As you scale from your current stage, what is the biggest "trust gap" your customers have with the product today? Is it a lack of features, or is it a need for better reliability and data integrity?
*   You are hiring across GTM and Engineering simultaneously. How are you ensuring that the product roadmap stays ahead of the promises being made by the sales team?
