# Interview Cheat Sheet: Atlassian (v1.0)
## Role: Product Manager, Developer Experience (DX)
## Context: Developer Tools Ecosystem / Internal Developer Platforms (IDP)

### 📋 1. The Strategy: "Removing Cognitive Load"
- **The "Pain":** Developers at scale are overwhelmed by tool fragmentation and "Self-Service" hurdles. They spend too much time on "Plumbing" and not enough time on "Shipping."
- **The Solution:** You are the DX PM who treats "Productivity" as a first-class citizen. You've done this at Cision by simplifying the **CPRE** infrastructure and bypassing the legacy ETL mess in **Bellwether**.

### 🔗 2. Tactical "Jason Bridges"
- **Bridge 1: Platform Engineering Mindset.**
  - *Context:* Managing CPRE infrastructure and technical integrations at Cision.
  - *Value:* Atlassian is moving toward Compass and unified platform experiences. You understand how to build shared services that developers actually want to use.
- **Bridge 2: High-Velocity Prioritization.**
  - *Context:* Using T-shirt sizing and capacity modeling for 15 engineers across 3 pods.
  - *Value:* Atlassian values "Heart and Balance." Your disciplined approach to capacity planning ensures developers are productive without being burned out by over-commitment.
- **Bridge 3: Reliability & DevSecOps.**
  - *Context:* Resolving 90% of a 300+ item security backlog.
  - *Value:* A good DX includes built-in security. Your experience remediating technical debt for a system of record proves you can integrate "Compliance" into the developer workflow.

### 🎯 3. Anticipated Technical Questions
- **"How do you measure the value of a Developer Experience product?"**
  - *Focus:* Developer Velocity, Deployment Frequency, and Team Sentitment. Mention your experience triaging support tickets and CX channels at Cision to find systemic friction points.
- **"Tell me about a time you had to influence a highly technical engineering team."**
  - *Focus:* Bellwether. Detail how you used data evidence (VOC reports showing 40% data loss) to convince the team to abandon the legacy ETL and build a direct-source bypass.
- **"How do you handle 'Stakeholder Management' when everyone has a different idea of 'Productivity'?"**
  - *Focus:* The "Initiative Stream" Filter. Explain how you created three primary streams at Cision and rejected anything that didn't align, ensuring focus and clarity for the team.

### 🕵️ 4. Reverse-Interview Questions (Strategic)
1. "As Atlassian moves toward more 'Unified Platform' experiences (Compass, Atlassian Intelligence), how are we ensuring that 'Generative AI' adds to developer productivity rather than adding more 'Management Overhead' to the workflow?"
2. "How do we define the 'Minimum Viable Experience' for a developer tool—is it about getting to the first deployment or about long-term maintainability?"
3. "Atlassian has a famous 'No Bullshit' culture. How does the DX team use that transparency to gather feedback from the internal and external developer communities?"

### ⚠️ 5. Red Flags to Avoid
- *Do NOT* claim you are a software engineer; emphasize your **Technical Product and Developer Experience** expertise.
- *Do NOT* use "vibe" words like 'passionate' or 'innovative'. Stick to **Productivity, Reliability, and Clarity**.
- *Do NOT* mention the "Context Firewall" (hallucination). Stick to **Bellwether** and **CPRE**.
