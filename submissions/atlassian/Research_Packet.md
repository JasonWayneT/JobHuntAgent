# Research Packet: Atlassian (v2.0)
## Role: Product Manager, Developer Experience (DX)

### Module A: The Company DNA
- **Core Mission/Values:**
  - **Stated:** "Unleash the potential of every team." Values: "Open company, no bullshit," "Build with heart and balance," "Don’t #@!% the customer."
  - **Observed:** Transitioning from a suite of tools to a unified "Atlassian Platform" where APIs and developer experience are the glue.
- **Problem Space:** Teams are fragmented across too many tools. Atlassian provides the "Collaboration OS" (Jira, Confluence, Bitbucket) that connects them.
- **Business Model:** B2B SaaS. High-velocity, low-touch sales model driven by product excellence and community.
- **Founding Story:** Founded in 2002 in Sydney by Mike Cannon-Brookes and Scott Farquhar with a $10,000 credit card debt.

### Module B: Market & Competitors
- **The "Big 3" Competitors:**
  - **GitHub / Microsoft:** The primary rival in the developer tools and CI/CD space.
  - **GitLab:** A strong "All-in-one" DevSecOps platform competitor.
  - **ServiceNow:** Competing in the enterprise IT Service Management (ITSM) and workflow space.
- **Atlassian Differentiator:** The "Ecosystem." Atlassian’s strength is not just the products, but the massive integration marketplace and the ubiquity of Jira as the industry standard.
- **Macro Trends:** The "Platform Engineering" wave. Companies are building internal developer platforms (IDPs) to reduce cognitive load on developers.

### Module C: Recent Intelligence (90-Day Window)
- **Financials:** Strong focus on cloud migration and enterprise expansion.
- **Organizational Shifts:** Significant investment in "Atlassian Intelligence" (AI) to automate common developer and team workflows.
- **Product News:** Launching "Compass," a developer experience portal to help teams track services and software health.

### Module D: The Role & Org Chart
- **JD Analysis:**
  - **Keywords:** Developer Experience (DX), Product Strategy, User Research, Data Analysis.

- **Operational Style:** **Remote-first** with highly autonomous, cross-functional squads.
