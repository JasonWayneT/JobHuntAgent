# Interview Cheat Sheet: Roadie (v1.0)
## Role: Product Manager (Last-Mile Delivery Platform)
## Context: Crowdsourced Logistics Marketplace / Supply Chain Tech

### 📋 1. The Strategy: "The Elastic Supply Chain"
- **The "Pain":** Traditional shipping is slow and rigid. Businesses need highly flexible, last-mile options that can handle everything from small documents to oversized lumber. This requires a "zero-outage," perfectly coordinated matching engine.
- **The Solution:** You are the platform integrity PM who protects the "Core" while enabling discovery. You've done this at Cision by diagnosing and solving the 40% data failure in the **Bellwether Project**.

### 🔗 2. Tactical "Jason Bridges"
- **Bridge 1: Marketplace Integrity.**
  - *Context:* The Bellwether Project at Cision (30-40% data drop-off resolution).
  - *Value:* A delivery marketplace is only as good as its data (driver locations, package sizes, SLAs). You have a proven track record of identifying and repairing structural "leaks" in data ingestion flows.
- **Bridge 2: Enterprise Reliability.**
  - *Context:* Managing $40M ARR platforms (C3/CPRE).
  - *Value:* Roadie is now a UPS company. Your experience managing high-revenue legacy platforms under enterprise security requirements (the 300-item backlog) proves you can operate at the "UPS standard."
- **Bridge 3: Operational Velocity.**
  - *Context:* Leading three engineering pods and using T-shirt sizing for 15 engineers.
  - *Value:* Roadie is a "fast-paced, high-growth environment." You don't just "talk" roadmap; you use capacity modeling to ensure the team ships and launches high-impact features on time.

### 🎯 3. Anticipated Technical Questions
- **"How do you approach 'Discovery' in a two-sided marketplace (Drivers vs. Senders)?"**
  - *Focus:* Stakeholder Synthesis. Explain how you'd triage bug tickets (as you did at Cision) and support channels for both sides to identify systemic friction points.
- **"Tell me about a time you used data to drive a major product decision."**
  - *Focus:* Bellwether. Detail the investigation showing 40% data loss and how that evidence persuaded the team to abandon the legacy pipeline for a direct-source integration.
- **"How do you measure success for a last-mile delivery feature?"**
  - *Focus:* Cycle Time, Delivery Accuracy, and Cost. Connect this to your experience looking backwards from "financial outcomes" (like churn reduction and revenue protection at Cision).

### 🕵️ 4. Reverse-Interview Questions (Strategic)
1. "As Roadie integrates more deeply with UPS, how are we balancing the 'Startup Velocity' of the Roadie platform with the 'Enterprise Reliability' expectations of a global conglomerate like UPS?"
2. "How do we define success for a Product Manager at Roadie—is it the 'Volume of deliveries initiated' or the 'Reduction in delivery failures/re-routes'?"
3. "Last-mile is highly localized. How is the product team leveraging hyper-local data to improve 'Route Optimization' in complex urban environments?"

### ⚠️ 5. Red Flags to Avoid
- *Do NOT* claim you are a logistics/trucking expert; focus on **Marketplace Integrity, Platform Stabilization, and Technical Delivery**.
- *Do NOT* use "vibe" words like 'passionate' or 'innovative'. Stick to **Reliability, Data, and Discipline**.
- *Do NOT* mention the "Context Firewall" (hallucination). Stick to **Bellwether** and **CPRE**.
