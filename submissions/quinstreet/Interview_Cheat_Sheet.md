# Interview Cheat Sheet: QuinStreet (v1.0)
## Role: Senior Associate Product Manager
## Context: Performance Marketplace / Fintech Matching Engines

### 📋 1. The Strategy: "The Accuracy of Matching"
- **The "Pain":** Consumers in the fintech comparison space want instant, accurate matches. Inaccurate offer experiences or friction-heavy onboarding leads to high bounce rates and lost revenue per user.
- **The Solution:** You are the technical PM who understands how to build reliable "Comparison Engines." You've done this at Cision by diagnosing and solving the 40% data failure in the **Bellwether Project**.

### 🔗 2. Tactical "Jason Bridges"
- **Bridge 1: Marketplace & Ingestion Integrity.**
  - *Context:* The Bellwether Project at Cision (30-40% drop-off resolution).
  - *Value:* QuinStreet's success depends on the "billions of dollars of online media experience" and its data algorithms. You have a proven track record of ensuring ingestion engines (like the one you built for Bellwether) are accurate.
- **Bridge 2: Revenue Retention.**
  - *Context:* Protecting $40M ARR and reducing churn for 3,500 accounts.
  - *Value:* The JD focuses on "conversion, engagement, and revenue." You are not just a "feature PM"; you are an "outcome PM" who understands the financial impact of technical stability.
- **Bridge 3: Technical-Business Coordination.**
  - *Context:* Leading three engineering pods and using T-shirt sizing for 15 engineers.
  - *Value:* QuinStreet values "Partnering with Design to simplify experiences." You are the "Bridge" who translates engineering constraints into simplified user journeys that convert.

### 🎯 3. Anticipated Technical Questions
- **"How do you approach experimentation in a high-traffic environment?"**
  - *Focus:* Hypothesis-Driven Testing. Explain how you'd look for "Drop-off points" (similar to how you found the indexing failure at Cision) and run phased experiments to test optimizations without breaking the core revenue.
- **"What does 'Data Integrity' mean for a matching engine?"**
  - *Focus:* Trust and Accuracy. Detail your Bellwether experience: identifying a systemic failure in the ETL chain and proposing a direct-source solution to ensure the "Matches" (or media contacts) were actually correct.
- **"Tell me about a time you took ownership from idea to execution."**
  - *Focus:* Bellwether or Migration Tooling. Detail the "Idea" (the direct bypass), the "Execution" (coordinating the eng pods), and the "Result" (revenue protected).

### 🕵️ 4. Reverse-Interview Questions (Strategic)
1. "QuinStreet has '20 years of online media experience.' How are we leveraging that massive historical data set with AI to improve matching accuracy beyond what our direct competitors like LendingTree can offer?"
2. "How do we define success for a 'Senior Associate PM'—is it the 'Number of experiments run' or the 'Revenue Per User' improvement on a specific marketplace vertical?"
3. "As we expand into new product verticals, how are we ensuring that our 'Onboarding Flow' remains simple for consumers while still gathering the complex data needed for accurate financial matching?"

### ⚠️ 5. Red Flags to Avoid
- *Do NOT* claim you are a fintech expert; focus on **Data Integrity, Marketplace Dynamics, and Platform Stabilization**.
- *Do NOT* use "vibe" words like 'passionate' or 'innovative'. Stick to **Accuracy, Conversion, and Performance**.
- *Do NOT* mention the "Context Firewall" (hallucination). Stick to **Bellwether** and **CPRE**.
