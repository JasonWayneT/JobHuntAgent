# Interview Cheat Sheet: Federal Express Corporation (v1.0)
## Role: Technical Product Manager III (Commercial Data & Insights)
## Context: Global Logistics Data Ecosystem

### 📋 1. The Strategy: "The Global Source of Truth"
- **The "Pain":** Moving data is as hard as moving packages. Legacy pipelines and fragmented integrations create blind spots in tracking and commercial insights.
- **The Solution:** You are the API and Data Integrity expert who knows how to bypass brittle architecture to create a unified, reliable feed. You've done this at Cision by solving the 40% data drop-off with **Project Bellwether**.

### 🔗 2. Tactical "Jason Bridges"
- **Bridge 1: API & Webhook Reliability.**
  - *Context:* Managing C3/CPRE and the **Bellwether Project** direct integrations.
  - *Value:* You understand idempotency, latency, and event-driven architecture. You know how to ensure a technical service is viable for enterprise-scale consumers.
- **Bridge 2: Risk-Based Prioritization.**
  - *Context:* Resolving 90% of a 300+ item security and pen-test backlog.
  - *Value:* FedEx is a critical infrastructure provider. Your experience balancing security technical debt with feature development is exactly what a TPM III needs to navigate a massive platform.
- **Bridge 3: Operational Stability.**
  - *Context:* Preventing index corruption outages by implementing proactive storage monitoring.
  - *Value:* You don't just ship features; you protect uptime. You know how to build the "monitoring and alerting" layer that keeps a global system healthy.

### 🎯 3. Anticipated Technical Questions
- **"How do you handle technical debt in a high-scale environment?"**
  - *Focus:* Risk-Weighted Triage. Cite the security backlog at Cision. Explain how you chunked the 300 items over a year to ensure stability without halting the roadmap.
- **"Describe a time you solved a major data integrity problem."**
  - *Focus:* Bellwether. Detail the 30-40% data loss in the legacy ETL chain and how you proposed a direct-source bypass to restore data trust.
- **"What is your approach to defining success for an API-based product?"**
  - *Focus:* Latency, Error Rates, and Developer Adoption. Mention your experience looking backwards from "financial outcomes" (like at Cision) to ensure the API supports the core business.

### 🕵️ 4. Reverse-Interview Questions (Strategic)
1. "As FedEx consolidates into 'One FedEx,' how is the Commercial Data & Insights platform evolving to support a more unified, global tracking experience?"
2. "How do we balance the requirement for 'zero-failure' reliability with the need for rapid API iteration in the Commercial Data space?"
3. "What does success look like for a TPM III in this role over the next 12 months—is it about platform expansion or technical debt reduction?"

### ⚠️ 5. Red Flags to Avoid
- *Do NOT* claim you are a logistics/shipping expert; emphasize your **Technical Platform and API Integrity** expertise.
- *Do NOT* use "vibe" words like 'passionate' or 'innovative'. Stick to **Reliability, Security, and Scale**.
- *Do NOT* mention the "Context Firewall" (hallucination). Stick to **Bellwether** and **CPRE**.
