# Research Packet: Federal Express Corporation (v2.0)
## Role: Technical Product Manager III (Commercial Data & Insights)

### Module A: The Company DNA
- **Core Mission/Values:**
  - **Stated:** "Connecting people and possibilities." Focus on safety, integrity, and service.
  - **Observed:** Transforming from a traditional logistics giant into a data-driven technology company. "Data is the new fuel" for global logistics.
- **Problem Space:** Logistics complexity at a global scale. FedEx needs to move data (tracking, telemetry, billing) as efficiently as it moves planes and trucks.
- **Business Model:** Enterprise Logistics & Transportation. Revenue from shipping services, supply chain solutions, and e-commerce enablement.
- **Founding Story:** Founded in 1971 by Fred Smith; revolutionized the industry with the "hub-and-spoke" system for overnight delivery.

### Module B: Market & Competitors
- **The "Big 3" Competitors:**
  - **UPS:** Direct rival. Moving fast into e-commerce and high-tech delivery (e.g., Roadie).
  - **DHL:** Strong international presence, especially in Europe and Asia.
  - **Amazon Logistics:** A rising threat that transitioned from a customer to a competitor by building its own delivery network.
- **FedEx Differentiator:** The "Purple Promise." Unmatched global air network and a strong focus on high-reliability enterprise data integrations.
- **Macro Trends:** The "Digitization of the Package." Customers demand real-time, API-driven visibility into every mile of the journey.

### Module C: Recent Intelligence (90-Day Window)
- **Financials:** Focus on cost reduction and network consolidation (Project DRIVE) to improve margins.
- **Organizational Shifts:** Merging FedEx Express and FedEx Ground into "One FedEx" to simplify the organization.
- **Product News:** Significant investment in "FedEx Dataworks" to leverage AI and machine learning for supply chain optimization.
