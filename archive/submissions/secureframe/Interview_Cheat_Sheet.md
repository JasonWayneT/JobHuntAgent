# Interview Run of Show: Jason Taylor x Secureframe

## 1. "Tell me about yourself" (The Platform Pitch)

I am a Product Manager with over six years of experience in B2B SaaS, specifically focused on the infrastructure and data integrity that keeps revenue-bearing platforms alive. Most recently at Cision, I managed a legacy platform generating 40 million dollars in annual revenue. My job was to stabilize that system while the company transitioned toward a newer architecture. 

I specialize in the unglamorous but critical parts of the product: data ingestion pipelines, platform reliability, and migration strategy. I am not a growth-hacker. I am the person you brought in when the data is stale, the architecture is complex, and the cost of failure is high. At Secureframe, you are automating trust and compliance. That requires the same obsession with data integrity and system reliability that I have spent my career refining. I know how to operate inside technical constraints and deliver outcomes that protect the bottom line.

## 2. Why Secureframe?

Secureframe is solving a fundamental trust problem for B2B SaaS companies. Compliance is often seen as a checkbox, but it is actually a data orchestration challenge. You are pulling evidence from dozens of disparate sources to prove a state of security. 

My interest in this role stems from two things. First, your mission of "automation backed by experts" resonates with my experience. I have spent years as a subject matter expert bridging the gap between complex technical systems and business requirements. Second, Secureframe is at a stage where scaling the platform requires disciplined prioritization. I have managed roadmaps through layoffs, leadership churn, and shifting corporate strategies. I know how to keep a team focused on high-leverage work when the environment is fast-paced or uncertain. I want to apply that discipline to a product that is actively unlocking growth for its customers.

## 3. Key Experience Bridge

**The Bellwether Project x Evidence Collection**
Secureframe relies on automated evidence collection. If the data from an integrated source is stale or missing, the trust in your platform breaks. At Cision, our biggest churn driver was stale contact data caused by 40 percent data loss in legacy ETL pipelines. I led the Bellwether initiative to bypass that broken plumbing and integrate directly with the source of truth. This restored data integrity and eliminated the primary reason customers were leaving. I can bring that same focus on "source of truth" ingestion to Secureframe’s automated evidence collectors.

**CPRE Infrastructure x Compliance Scaling**
Compliance is not a point-in-time event; it is a continuous state. I managed CPRE, the underlying infrastructure for a platform with 25,000 active users. I handled the trade-offs between shipping new features and resolving a 300-item security backlog. I understand how to prioritize infrastructure work that reduces risk without stalling the product roadmap. This is directly applicable to Secureframe as you scale your platform to support more complex certifications and larger enterprise clients.

## 4. Handling Ambiguity and Stability

In a startup environment like Secureframe, there is a constant tension between moving fast and maintaining a stable, compliant platform. My approach to this is rooted in capacity discipline. 

At Cision, I moved the team away from reactive planning toward a structured capacity model. We used T-shirt sizing and uncertainty bands to account for the "known unknowns" of legacy systems. I allocated specific buckets for unplanned work, security debt, and strategic initiatives. This allowed us to maintain a stable platform even as engineering headcount fluctuated. 

When things are ambiguous, I focus on the "plumbing." If the data ingestion is reliable and the system architecture is transparent, the business can pivot its strategy more easily. I don't need perfect conditions to ship. I build the systems that make shipping predictable.

## 5. Custom Reverse-Interview Questions

**For the CTO / Engineering Leadership:**
* Secureframe promises "automation backed by world-class experts." As the platform scales, how do you prevent the "expert" side from becoming a bottleneck for the "automation" side? 
* When you are integrating with dozens of third-party APIs for evidence collection, how do you manage the trade-off between the breadth of integrations and the depth of data reliability for each one?
* What is the current state of your "security debt" versus your "feature roadmap," and how do you decide when a platform stability issue takes precedence over a new compliance module?

**For the CEO / Product Leadership:**
* You are moving from point-in-time audits to continuous compliance. How has that shift changed the way your customers perceive the value of the platform, and what is the biggest hurdle to making that the industry standard?
* Secureframe operates in a crowded market with competitors like Vanta and Drata. Beyond just "more integrations," what is the one architectural or data-level differentiator that you believe will win the enterprise market?
* Given the current macro environment for SaaS, are you seeing a shift in customer demand toward "cost-saving" compliance or "revenue-unlocking" compliance? How does that change our prioritization for the next twelve months?
