# Interview Run of Show: Jason Taylor x Allstate
## Role: Digital Product Manager (Platform / Container Workloads)

### 1. "Tell me about yourself" (60-90 Seconds)
I am a Platform Product Manager with over six years of experience managing technically complex, revenue-bearing B2B SaaS environments. My career has focused on the intersection of legacy stability and modernization. Most recently at Cision, I owned the C3 and CPRE platforms, which accounted for approximately $40M in ARR. 

My specialty is operating within constraints. I have managed platforms through multiple layoff cycles and executive leadership changes while maintaining a 7% churn rate. I do not just build features. I build the underlying systems that ensure data integrity and platform reliability. For example, I led the Bellwether initiative to bypass failing legacy ETL pipelines and integrate directly with upstream sources of truth. This eliminated a 40% data drop off and resolved our primary churn driver. 

I am here because Allstate is currently scaling its digital product footprint, specifically in container workloads and infrastructure. You need someone who understands how to protect a massive, mature business while migrating it toward modern architecture without breaking the revenue model.

### 2. Why Allstate?
Allstate is moving from a traditional insurance giant to a digital-first protection company. Your current focus on container workloads and M365 indicates a massive shift in how you handle internal and external service delivery. 

I am interested in this role because I have spent the last four years at Cision doing exactly this type of work. I managed the CPRE infrastructure that supported 25,000 active users. I understand the friction that occurs when a mature organization tries to modernize its stack. My experience with migration tooling and voluntary, phased rollouts aligns with Allstate’s mission to keep customers protected and prepared. You cannot afford "move fast and break things" in insurance. You need disciplined, platform-level product management that ensures the transition to containers or new design systems is invisible to the end user but transformative for the business.

### 3. Key Experience Bridge
**The Challenge: Data Integrity and Risk**
*   **Allstate Context:** Insurance relies on accurate, real-time data to assess risk and protect customers.
*   **Jason’s Bridge (Bellwether):** At Cision, our media database was the core product, but it was stale. I identified that 30 to 40 percent of data was being lost in legacy plumbing. I proposed and executed an architectural bypass to sync directly with the source of truth. I can apply this same "source of truth" discipline to Allstate’s data ingestion pipelines to ensure the protection products are based on the most current information available.

**The Challenge: Infrastructure as a Product**
*   **Allstate Context:** The Digital PM (Container Workloads) role treats internal infrastructure as a product for other developers.
*   **Jason’s Bridge (CPRE/DT Search):** I managed CPRE, the infrastructure layer for C3. When our indexing engine, DT Search, kept failing due to storage exhaustion, I did not just ask for more disk space. I implemented monitoring and partnered with legal to purge dead accounts. I treated the infrastructure's stability as a retention feature. I understand how to manage a roadmap where the "customers" are often internal engineering teams.

### 4. Handling Ambiguity & Stability
In a mature organization like Allstate, the hardest part of the job is often balancing the "new" with the "legacy." At Cision, I operated with a shrinking headcount and shifting priorities. 

I handle this through disciplined capacity modeling. I do not guess what a team can do. I calculate workdays, subtract PTO, and use T-shirt sizing with uncertainty bands to create a realistic roadmap. When layoffs happened, I consolidated three teams into one and focused on three specific initiative streams: security, stability, and migration. 

If a request did not fit those streams, the answer was no. This level of discipline is required when you are managing a platform that is "supposed" to be sunsetting but is still paying the bills. I can bring that same pragmatic prioritization to Allstate’s digital transformation.
