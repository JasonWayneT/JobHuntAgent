# Interview Run of Show: Jason Taylor x Ryan

## 1. "Tell me about yourself" (60-90 Seconds)

I am a Platform Product Manager who specializes in managing technically complex, revenue-bearing B2B SaaS environments. My career has been defined by operating inside constraints like legacy architecture, shifting organizational priorities, and shrinking resources while still delivering stability and data integrity.

Most recently at Cision, I owned the legacy C3 platform and its underlying infrastructure, CPRE. This was a forty million dollar ARR ecosystem that supported thirty-five hundred global accounts. My mandate was not high-growth feature development. It was platform stabilization, protecting revenue from churn, and modernizing data pipelines. I led the Bellwether initiative, which bypassed failing legacy ETL chains to restore data trust for twenty-five thousand users.

I am not a greenfield or consumer-facing PM. I am at my best when I am sitting close to engineering and DevOps to solve "plumbing" problems that have massive business implications. I understand how to balance the need for new scalable capabilities with the reality of maintaining the legacy systems that currently pay the bills. I am here because Ryan is at a similar inflection point: reconciling a massive portfolio of data-heavy tax products into a unified, scalable platform.

## 2. Why Ryan?

Ryan is a global leader in tax services that is effectively transforming into a software-first organization. Your current challenge is not just building apps. It is building a reusable platform that can handle the massive data requirements of Global 5000 tax compliance. 

My experience at Cision aligns directly with this. I spent four years managing the tension between legacy systems and the push toward a unified data architecture. I understand the risks of forced migrations and the necessity of building data integrity into the platform layer rather than the application layer. 

Ryan needs PMs who can make decisions with incomplete data and who have the technical literacy to talk to engineers about APIs and ingestion pipelines. I have spent my career doing exactly that in environments where the cost of failure is high and the data is messy.

## 3. Key Experience Bridge

**Ryan Challenge: Data Integrity and Scalability**
Ryan serves the Global 5000. In tax compliance, stale or dropped data is a legal and financial liability. You need a platform that ensures data quality across multiple segments like indirect and property tax.

*   **The Bridge (Project Bellwether):** At Cision, our biggest churn driver was stale contact data caused by a thirty to forty percent drop-off in our legacy ETL pipeline. I proposed and led Bellwether, an architectural bypass that integrated directly with the upstream source of truth. This eliminated the data drop-off and restored customer trust. I can apply this same "bypass and simplify" logic to Ryan’s data-heavy tax applications to ensure compliance accuracy.

**Ryan Challenge: Reconciling Legacy with New Capabilities**
The JD emphasizes building a reusable platform while maintaining existing software for SMBs and large multinationals.

*   **The Bridge (Migration Tooling & CPRE):** I managed the sunset phase of a legacy platform while enabling voluntary, phased migrations. We built structured tooling to move accounts based on feature parity rather than arbitrary deadlines. I know how to protect forty million dollars in recurring revenue while simultaneously preparing the infrastructure for the next generation of products.

## 4. Handling Ambiguity & Stability

Ryan is a "dynamic, rapidly expanding" firm. In PM language, that often means shifting requirements and high ambiguity. 

*   **The Strategy:** I do not wait for perfect conditions or clean data to act. At Cision, I survived multiple leadership changes and layoff cycles by instituting a disciplined prioritization system. 
*   **The Proof:** When our support organization was disbanded, I built a structured Jira scoring system based on revenue impact and severity to keep the engineering team focused. I use capacity modeling based on actual workdays and uncertainty bands to ensure we ship what we promise. 
*   **The Message:** I am a "stabilizer" PM. I can walk into a chaotic technical environment, map the dependencies, and create a roadmap that engineers trust and stakeholders can bank on.
