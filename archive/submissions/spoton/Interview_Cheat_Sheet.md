## 3. Key Experience Bridge: Cision Stories to SpotOn Challenges

My experience at Cision directly addresses several critical areas for SpotOn's Payments Product Manager role:

*   **Platform Reliability & Performance (SpotOn: Authorization Rates, Latency)**: At Cision, I mitigated recurring indexing server crashes on our core B2B SaaS platform, implementing proactive monitoring and cleanups that reduced overall service outages. This experience in ensuring uptime and performance for a $40 million ARR platform is directly transferable to optimizing payment authorization rates, reducing latency, and maintaining the high availability essential for a payments system.
*   **Data Integrity & Reconciliation Accuracy (SpotOn: Reconciliation Accuracy, Funding Transparency)**: I conceived and drove a centralized platform data remediation initiative that bypassed failing legacy ETL paths, eliminating a 30-40% data drop-off rate and reducing stale-data complaints to zero. This demonstrates a proven ability to ensure absolute data accuracy and integrity, which is critical for flawless payment reconciliation, accurate merchant funding, and maintaining trust in a financial ecosystem.
*   **Regulatory Compliance & Risk Management (SpotOn: PCI DSS Adherence, Risk)**: I prioritized a massive backlog of approximately 300 penetration test vulnerabilities, resolving 90% of high-priority security risks over a year. This pragmatic approach to managing and mitigating security and compliance risks is directly relevant to navigating PCI DSS adherence, payment network rules, and partnering effectively with SpotOn's risk and compliance teams.
*   **Complex Migrations & Stakeholder Alignment (SpotOn: Evolving Payment Methods, External Partners)**: I partnered with upgrades, engineering, and customer experience teams to build and deploy phased voluntary migration tooling, successfully transitioning approximately 700 high-risk accounts without customer friction. This showcases my ability to manage complex, revenue-critical transitions and align diverse stakeholders, which is essential when integrating new payment partners or evolving payment acceptance strategies.

## 4. Handling Ambiguity & Stability

My career, particularly at Cision, involved operating in highly ambiguous and constrained environments. I managed a $40 million ARR legacy platform that was slated for sunset, under conditions of shrinking engineering headcount, multiple layoff cycles, and executive turnover. This context forced a pragmatic approach to product management.

Rather than being paralyzed by ambiguity, I learned to identify the highest leverage points for impact. This meant rigorously prioritizing initiatives that directly addressed platform stability, mitigated critical risks, or ensured data integrity, even when resources were scarce. For example, my work on platform stabilization and data remediation was not about greenfield innovation, but about ensuring the core business remained operational and reliable for its 25,000 users.

This experience instilled a deep discipline in capacity modeling and scope control. It taught me to balance the need for continuous improvement and strategic evolution with the non-negotiable requirement for stability and compliance. In a fast-paced, growth-stage fintech like SpotOn, where innovation must coexist with the critical need for payment reliability and regulatory rigor, this ability to deliver tangible results under pressure is a core strength.
