## 3. Key Experience Bridge

### Bellwether: Restoring Data Trust in Critical Services

**Cision Context:** The single largest customer complaint and churn driver for our $40M ARR legacy C3 platform was stale contact data. An investigation revealed a 30-40% data drop-off due to a convoluted, unowned legacy ETL pipeline. Instead of patching, I proposed and led the "Bellwether" initiative: a direct architectural bypass to GPOD, the upstream source of truth. This involved a full data sync and ongoing delta ingestion, eliminating the data loss and resolving the primary stale-data complaint. While I cannot quantify revenue retained, it directly addressed the biggest product-driven churn risk.

**Ad Hoc Connection:** This experience directly maps to Ad Hoc's challenge of modernizing outdated government services and improving service reliability. Government systems often suffer from fragmented, unreliable data. My ability to diagnose a critical user pain point, simplify complex data flows through targeted architectural intervention, and deliver a tangible improvement in data quality and user trust is highly relevant. It demonstrates an outcomes-focused approach to improving foundational service functionality, which is paramount for VA digital experiences.

### CPRE Infrastructure & Stability: Modernizing Under Constraint

**Cision Context:** I was responsible for CPRE, the underlying infrastructure for our legacy C3 platform. This environment was aging, and my mandate was to ensure stability, security, and cost control while resources were shrinking. For example, we addressed recurring outages caused by DT Search storage exhaustion by implementing monitoring and removing dead accounts, significantly reducing service disruptions. We also resolved approximately 90% of a 300-item security backlog through prioritized, phased remediation. I initiated the scoping for a Kafka migration to improve content pipeline scalability and reliability, and integrated C3 with UCP for unified content ingestion. This work was done amidst multiple layoffs and leadership changes.

**Ad Hoc Connection:** This experience is directly applicable to Ad Hoc's work transforming public services. Government systems are frequently legacy, technically complex, and require careful modernization without disrupting critical operations. My track record of stabilizing critical systems, improving reliability, managing security backlogs, and optimizing costs under constraint demonstrates the ability to operate effectively within environments characterized by technical debt. It shows I can prioritize foundational service quality and security, which are non-negotiable for government services, especially those supporting Veterans.
