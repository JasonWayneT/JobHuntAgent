# Interview Cheat Sheet: Rafay (v1.0)
## Role: Product Manager (GPU PaaS & Cloud-Native Automation)
## Context: Multi-Cloud / Kubernetes Orchestration / AI Infrastructure

### 📋 1. The Strategy: "The Governance Layer for AI"
- **The "Pain":** Enterprises want to scale AI, but they are drowning in the complexity of managing GPU clusters and Kubernetes across multiple clouds. It is insecure, manual, and brittle.
- **The Solution:** You are the platform-focused PM who knows how to build the "Automation and Guardrails" that make infrastructure self-service. You've done this at Cision by stabilizing the **CPRE** foundations and simplifying the **Bellwether** data flow.

### 🔗 2. Tactical "Jason Bridges"
- **Bridge 1: Infrastructure-as-a-Product.**
  - *Context:* Managing CPRE and technical integrations at Cision.
  - *Value:* Rafay is building a "Platform for Platforms." You understand that your customers are other platform teams and developers. You know how to define requirements for core services (APIs, Monitoring, Security).
- **Bridge 2: Security & Compliance Excellence.**
  - *Context:* Resolving 90% of a 300+ item security and pen-test backlog.
  - *Value:* Rafay targets "Cloud providers and enterprise platform teams" who care deeply about security. Your experience remediating technical debt for a $40M ARR system proves you prioritize the "Safety" of the automation.
- **Bridge 3: Agile Execution at Technical Scale.**
  - *Context:* Leading three engineering pods and using T-shirt sizing for 15 engineers.
  - *Value:* Rafay values a "Strong bias for action." Your disciplined approach to capacity planning and roadmap execution ensures you ship impactful features without breaking the "current" system.

### 🎯 3. Anticipated Technical Questions
- **"How do you approach productizing a technical feature for a developer audience?"**
  - *Focus:* Self-Service and Reliability. Mention your experience implementing monitoring for DT Search indexing at Cision to prevent outages—making the system "Self-Healing."
- **"Tell me about a time you had to pivot a product strategy based on customer data."**
  - *Focus:* Bellwether. Explain how customer churn data (due to stale records) forced the decision to abandon the legacy ETL path in favor of a direct-source integration.
- **"What is your understanding of the 'GPU PaaS' landscape?"**
  - *Focus:* Orchestration and Cost. Mention that it's about making expensive resources (GPUs) accessible and governed. Connect this to your experience managing "Resource Extraction" and "Capacity Modeling" at Cision.

### 🕵️ 4. Reverse-Interview Questions (Strategic)
1. "As Rafay leans into the GPU PaaS market, how are we balancing the need for 'Deep NVIDIA Integration' with the requirement for 'Cloud-Agility' across AWS, Azure, and GCP?"
2. "How do we define success for the automation platform—is it about 'Cluster Uptime,' 'Provisioning Speed,' or 'Developer Adoption' of the self-service portal?"
3. "Rafay focuses on the 'Enterprise Platform Team.' How is the product roadmap evolving to support 'AI Developers' directly with self-service LLM infrastructure?"

### ⚠️ 5. Red Flags to Avoid
- *Do NOT* claim you are a Kubernetes expert; emphasize your **Technical Platform and Infrastructure Product** expertise.
- *Do NOT* use "vibe" words like 'passionate' or 'innovative'. Stick to **Reliability, Security, and Scale**.
- *Do NOT* mention the "Context Firewall" (hallucination). Stick to **Bellwether** and **CPRE**.
