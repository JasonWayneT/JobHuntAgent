# Interview Cheat Sheet: InformData (v1.0)
## Role: Product Manager, Criminal Operations
## Context: Criminal Records Data Service (DaaS)

### 📋 1. The Strategy: "The Direct Source Advantage"
- **The "Pain":** Background screening depends on fresh, accurate criminal data. Aggregate databases are often stale or incomplete, leading to legal risks for employers and screening agencies.
- **The Solution:** You are the data integrity PM who knows how to bypass aggregated silos to get to the "Direct Source." You've done this at Cision with the **Bellwether Project**.

### 🔗 2. Tactical "Jason Bridges"
- **Bridge 1: Data Integrity & ETL Bypass.**
  - *Context:* The Bellwether Project at Cision (30-40% data drop-off resolution).
  - *Value:* InformData's core value is direct-source data. You have a proven track record of identifying ETL bottlenecks and replacing them with direct, reliable ingestion paths.
- **Bridge 2: System of Record Responsibility.**
  - *Context:* Managing $40M ARR tied to Legacy C3 and CPRE infrastructure.
  - *Value:* Criminal data is sensitive. Your experience managing high-revenue legacy platforms under security audit conditions (the 300-item backlog) proves you can handle mission-critical data.
- **Bridge 3: Technical-Business Alignment.**
  - *Context:* Capacity modeling and Jira prioritization across 15 engineers.
  - *Value:* The JD mentions "Serves as a subject matter expert." You are a PM who understands the "Technicals" (APIs, Identifiers, ETLs) but focuses on the "Business" (Retention, Churn, ROI).

### 🎯 3. Anticipated Technical Questions
- **"How do you ensure data accuracy when dealing with thousands of external sources?"**
  - *Focus:* Monitoring, Alerting, and Audit Trails. Mention your experience at Cision implementing alerting for the DT Search indexing and how you used data evidence (VOC reports) to prove the need for Bellwether.
- **"Tell me about a time you had to lead a complex cross-functional project."**
  - *Focus:* Bellwether or the Security Backlog. Detail the stakeholders involved (Legal, Engineering, DevOps) and how you used structured prioritization (T-shirt sizing) to keep them aligned.
- **"What is your approach to managing a product backlog in an Agile environment?"**
  - *Focus:* Allocation Buckets. Explain how you balanced "New Data Acquisition" with "Technical Debt/Bugs" to ensure the platform remained stable for enterprise clients.

### 🕵️ 4. Reverse-Interview Questions (Strategic)
1. "As InformData moves toward more automated, AI-driven public record extraction, how are we ensuring that 'Automation' doesn't compromise the 'Accuracy' that is our core differentiator?"
2. "How do we define success for the Criminal Operations team—is it 'Time to Acquisition,' 'Data Freshness,' or 'Market Coverage'?"
3. "How does the product team interact with the courthouse research ops teams to ensure technical requirements reflect the reality of local record accessibility?"

### ⚠️ 5. Red Flags to Avoid
- *Do NOT* claim you are a criminal law expert; focus on **Data Integrity, Platform Stabilization, and ETL/API Management**.
- *Do NOT* use "vibe" words like 'passionate' or 'innovative'. Stick to **Accuracy, Reliability, and Discipline**.
- *Do NOT* mention the "Context Firewall" (hallucination). Stick to **Bellwether** and **CPRE**.
