# Interview Cheat Sheet: Kin Insurance (v1.0)
## Role: Data Product Manager, Growth Tech
## Context: InsurTech Conversion Funnel / Data Platform Engineering

### 📋 1. The Strategy: "The Conversion Integrity Layer"
- **The "Pain":** Kin's growth depends on quoting accuracy and conversion speed. Fragmented data across tools (Segment, HubSpot, FullStory) leads to "blind spots" where potential quotes drop off.
- **The Solution:** You are the data-driven PM who connects these dots to find the "Why" behind the drop-off. You've done this at Cision by diagnosing the 40% data failure in the **Bellwether Project**.

### 🔗 2. Tactical "Jason Bridges"
- **Bridge 1: Data Integration & ETL Mastery.**
  - *Context:* Bypassing legacy ETL pipelines in the Bellwether Project.
  - *Value:* Kin is moving toward more unified data flows (Segment/Databricks). You understand how brittle ingestion chains fail and how to build more direct alternatives that preserve data trust.
- **Bridge 2: Analytical-Technical Bridge.**
  - *Context:* Advanced SQL and capacity modeling for 15 engineers.
  - *Value:* The JD emphasizes Advanced SQL and the ability to "independently extract, join, and analyze data." You are a PM who doesn't wait for a data analyst; you pull your own evidence.
- **Bridge 3: Financial Outcome Focus.**
  - *Context:* Protecting $40M ARR and reducing churn for 3,500 accounts.
  - *Value:* Growth Tech isn't about features; it's about conversion. Your experience linking technical stability to revenue retention at Cision is a direct match for Kin's disciplined growth model.

### 🎯 3. Anticipated Technical Questions
- **"Tell me about a time you turned a fragmented data insight into a shipped feature."**
  - *Focus:* Migration Tooling. Cite your experience at Cision building phased tooling to enable migration for high-risk accounts. Explain how you used usage data to define the "Phasing" of the tool.
- **"How do you approach a multi-source data integrity problem (e.g., discrepancies between CRM and Policy data)?"**
  - *Focus:* The "Common Thread" approach. Explain how you used unique identifiers and proactive monitoring at Cision to identify storage exhaustion and indexing issues before they hit the customer.
- **"What is your approach to partnering with engineering teams to scope data products?"**
  - *Focus:* T-shirt sizing and clarity. Explain how you use structured requirement definition to ensure engineering doesn't build "Data for Data's Sake" but builds the specific pipes needed for growth.

### 🕵️ 4. Reverse-Interview Questions (Strategic)
1. "Kin is known for its disciplined unit economics. How does the Growth Tech team balance the high cost of data platform scaling (like Databricks) with the need for rapid experimentation in the conversion funnel?"
2. "How do we define success for a 'Data Product Manager'—is it the 'Cleanliness of the Data Lake' or the 'Conversion Rate improvement on the site'?"
3. "As Kin expands to more states, how are we evolving our 'Identity Resolution' (e.g., Segment) to handle increasingly complex multi-state customer behavior?"

### ⚠️ 5. Red Flags to Avoid
- *Do NOT* claim you are a growth marketing expert; emphasize your **Data Integrity, Platform Engineering, and Analytical PM** expertise.
- *Do NOT* use "vibe" words like 'passionate' or 'innovative'. Stick to **Conversion, Data, and Discipline**.
- *Do NOT* mention the "Context Firewall" (hallucination). Stick to **Bellwether** and **CPRE**.
