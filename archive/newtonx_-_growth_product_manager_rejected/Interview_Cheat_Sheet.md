# Interview Cheat Sheet: NewtonX (v1.0)
## Role: Growth Product Manager (Expert Community)
## Context: B2B Expert Marketplace / Acquisition Funnels

### 📋 1. The Strategy: "The High-Integrity Onboarding"
- **The "Pain":** NewtonX's growth depends on the quality and volume of its expert community. Friction in the onboarding flow or "drop-off" in the acquisition funnel limits the company's ability to fulfill high-value client research demands.
- **The Solution:** You are the funnel integrity PM who knows how to diagnose systemic drop-off. You've done this at Cision by solving the 40% data failure in the **Bellwether Project**.

### 🔗 2. Tactical "Jason Bridges"
- **Bridge 1: Funnel & Data Integrity.**
  - *Context:* The Bellwether Project at Cision (30-40% data drop-off resolution).
  - *Value:* A growth funnel is just a specialized data pipeline. You have a proven track record of identifying where "Data" (or users) are being lost and building structural solutions (like the direct-source bypass) to recover them.
- **Bridge 2: Experimentation & Hypothesis Testing.**
  - *Context:* Investigating platform-level outages caused by storage exhaustion and index corruption.
  - *Value:* NewtonX values a "hypothesis-driven approach." You approach system problems with scientific rigor. You didn't just fix the index; you built the monitoring and alerting to "prove" the fix.
- **Bridge 3: Technical-Cross-Functional Coordination.**
  - *Context:* Coordinating three engineering pods and aligning stakeholders for migration tooling.
  - *Value:* Growth PMs must bridge Product, Data, Marketing, and Operations. Your experience managing $40M ARR platforms and $22k/yr automation save initiatives at Sterkly proves you can speak all these languages.

### 🎯 3. Anticipated Technical Questions
- **"How do you prioritize growth experiments when you have limited engineering resources?"**
  - *Focus:* Potential Impact vs. Effort (T-shirt sizing). Explain how you used capacity modeling at Cision to ensure you balanced "New Experiments" with "Backlog Integrity" (like the 90% security remediation).
- **"Tell me about a time you had to optimize a complex user journey."**
  - *Focus:* Migration Tooling. Detail your experience at Cision building phased tooling for high-risk accounts. Explain how you used usage metrics to "Onboard" users to the new platform voluntarily.
- **"What does 'PLG' (Product-Led Growth) mean in a B2B marketplace context?"**
  - *Focus:* Removing Friction. Connect this to your experience at Zero To Sixty Media, where you automated onboarding workflows into Salesforce to "Save $22,100 in overhead." PLG is about making the tool sell itself.

### 🕵️ 4. Reverse-Interview Questions (Strategic)
1. "As NewtonX scales its 'Custom Recruiting Engine,' how are we ensuring that 'Automation' in the acquisition funnel doesn't compromise the 'Verification' that is our core value proposition to McKinsey and Microsoft?"
2. "How do we define success for the 'Expert Community' Growth PM—is it the 'Cost Per Lead,' the 'Onboarding Completion Rate,' or the 'Expert Participation Rate' in projects?"
3. "The JD mentions balancing 'quick wins' with 'scalable initiatives.' What is the biggest 'Scalability' bottleneck we currently face in the expert acquisition funnel?"

### ⚠️ 5. Red Flags to Avoid
- *Do NOT* claim you are a market research expert; focus on **Funnel Integrity, Platform Stabilization, and Growth Experimentation**.
- *Do NOT* use "vibe" words like 'passionate' or 'innovative'. Stick to **Conversion, Data, and Discipline**.
- *Do NOT* mention the "Context Firewall" (hallucination). Stick to **Bellwether** and **CPRE**.
