# Interview Cheat Sheet: Snapsheet Inc (v1.0)
## Role: Product Manager I - Foundational Services
## Context: InsurTech Core Infrastructure / Claims Engine

### 📋 1. The Strategy: "The Stable Engine"
- **The "Pain":** Insurance carriers cannot afford downtime or data loss in their claims process. Foundational Services must be invisible, reliable, and perfectly integrated with upstream/downstream systems.
- **The Solution:** You are the platform stabilizer who protects the "Core" while enabling discovery. You've done this at Cision with **CPRE** and the **Bellwether Project**.

### 🔗 2. Tactical "Jason Bridges"
- **Bridge 1: API & Foundational Services.**
  - *Context:* Managing CPRE and technical integrations at Cision.
  - *Value:* You understand that foundational services are products for internal and external developers. You know how to manage API contracts, technical debt, and architectural simplification.
- **Bridge 2: Data-Driven Prioritization.**
  - *Context:* Using capacity modeling and T-shirt sizing for 15 engineers.
  - *Value:* Snapsheet values "Rigorously leveraging data and analytics to drive decisions." You don't guess; you use evidence and capacity constraints to build a realistic roadmap.
- **Bridge 3: Technical Risk Mitigation.**
  - *Context:* Resolving 90% of a 300+ item security backlog.
  - *Value:* Claims data is highly sensitive (PII). Your experience in security remediation for a system of record proves you prioritize the "Safety and Integrity" of the platform.

### 🎯 3. Anticipated Technical Questions
- **"How do you balance foundational technical debt with new feature requests?"**
  - *Focus:* Allocation Buckets. Cite your Cision experience: allocating specific % of capacity to stability, security, and roadmap items to ensure the "Foundation" stays healthy.
- **"Tell me about a time you had to negotiate scope with stakeholders."**
  - *Focus:* T-shirt sizing and VUCA-style uncertainty bands. Explain how you communicated "what is possible" based on actual engineering hours to manage executive expectations.
- **"How do you approach product discovery for a technical/foundational service?"**
  - *Focus:* Internal Stakeholder & Client Interviews. Mention your experience triaging bug tickets and support channels to identify systemic patterns (like the stale data problem solved by Bellwether).

### 🕵️ 4. Reverse-Interview Questions (Strategic)
1. "As Snapsheet scales across 170+ carriers, how is the Foundational Services team evolving its 'Open Architecture' to support diverse carrier workflows without over-complicating the core engine?"
2. "How do we measure success for foundational services? Is it platform uptime, API latency, or the velocity of the feature teams that build on top of us?"
3. "What is the biggest 'Foundational' hurdle we face in the next 12 months—is it scalability for new volume or modularity for new product lines?"

### ⚠️ 5. Red Flags to Avoid
- *Do NOT* claim you are a P&C insurance expert; emphasize your **Platform Integrity and API-First PM** expertise.
- *Do NOT* use "vibe" words like 'passionate' or 'innovative'. Stick to **Reliability, Data, and Discipline**.
- *Do NOT* mention the "Context Firewall" (hallucination). Stick to **Bellwether** and **CPRE**.
