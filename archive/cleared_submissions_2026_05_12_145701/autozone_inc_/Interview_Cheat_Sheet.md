## 4. Handling Ambiguity & Stability

My career at Cision was defined by operating within significant ambiguity and instability. I managed a core B2B SaaS platform that, despite generating $40M ARR, was slated for sunset. This environment involved repeated layoffs, executive turnover, and a complete lack of dedicated customer support.

This context forced me to become a highly pragmatic problem solver. I learned to:
*   **Prioritize ruthlessly**: Focusing on the highest-impact initiatives that protected revenue and customer satisfaction, even when the overall platform direction was winding down.
*   **Drive consensus without formal authority**: Building trust and alignment with engineering managers, database administrators, and customer experience teams when direct leadership was in flux.
*   **Deliver measurable impact in constraint**: Achieving significant outcomes, such as eliminating 100% of stale-data complaints and resolving 90% of a security risk backlog, despite shrinking resources.
*   **Navigate legacy complexity**: Thriving in environments with imperfect data and layered legacy systems, finding architectural bypasses and stabilization strategies that delivered immediate value.

This experience has prepared me to contribute effectively at AutoZone, where the focus is on driving measurable impact within a complex, omnichannel retail environment. I am adept at identifying critical pain points, proposing pragmatic solutions, and executing with discipline, even when the path forward is not perfectly clear.
