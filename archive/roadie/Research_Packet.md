# Research Packet: Roadie (v2.0)
## Role: Product Manager (Last-Mile Delivery Platform)

### Module A: The Company DNA
- **Core Mission/Values:**
  - **Stated:** "Connecting businesses with items to send with drivers already on the road."
  - **Observed:** A "Crowdsourced Delivery" platform that leverages unused capacity in passenger vehicles to provide fast, flexible last-mile logistics.
- **Problem Space:** Last-mile delivery is the most expensive and complex part of the supply chain. Roadie provides a "Scalable, elastic" solution for businesses ranging from local florists to Home Depot.
- **Business Model:** B2B/B2C Marketplace. Acquired by UPS in 2021 as a strategic technology play for crowdsourced delivery.
- **Founding Story:** Founded in 2014 by Marc Gorlin to solve the "I forgot my keys across town" problem, evolving into a major enterprise logistics platform.

### Module B: Market & Competitors
- **The "Big 3" Competitors:**
  - **DoorDash / Uber Direct:** Moving aggressively into non-food delivery (retail, pharmacy, groceries).
  - **Amazon Flex:** Amazon's internal version of crowdsourced delivery for its own packages.
  - **FedEx / UPS (Legacy):** While Roadie is owned by UPS, it represents a different, more flexible model than the traditional "Brown Truck" delivery.
- **Roadie Differentiator:** "Long Haul and Oversized." Unlike DoorDash which focuses on small food items, Roadie handles everything from documents to massive lumber orders (Home Depot partnership).
- **Macro Trends:** The "E-commerce Decentralization." Retailers are shipping from stores (SFS) rather than warehouses to reduce time-to-customer.

### Module C: Recent Intelligence (90-Day Window)
- **Financials:** Strong backing as a wholly-owned subsidiary of UPS.
- **Organizational Shifts:** Integrating more deeply with UPS's global logistics network to support "Next Day" crowdsourced operations.
- **Product News:** Launching enhanced "Route Optimization" and "Real-time Tracking" features to improve driver efficiency.
